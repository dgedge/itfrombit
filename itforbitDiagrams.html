Visual representations of how fundamental 8-bit circlette codewords arrange on the 2D lattice. Each oriented ring represents a fundamental fermion; sectors encode quantum properties; connections represent shared parity checks (forces). Molecular geometry is not imposed by space—it emerges perfectly from the topology of bit alignment.

2. The Ring Layout & Interaction Sectors

The 8 bits are geometrically fixed around the ring. Because different physical forces mediate through different bit sectors, interacting particles must physically rotate on the lattice like puzzle pieces so their relevant sectors face each other.

Geometric Coupling: The strong force couples exclusively through the Colour bits (C₀, C₁). To bind, quarks must rotate on the 2D lattice so their colour sectors face the geometric centre of the bond. As a result, the Electroweak and Generation sectors point outward.

3. The Proton: Topological Colour Confinement

In a proton (uud), three quark circlettes are bound by the strong force. The parity check enforcing colour neutrality ($r \oplus g \oplus b = \text{white}$) connects the C₀, C₁ bits of each quark. For this check to be executed locally on the 2D lattice, all three rings must mechanically rotate so their colour sectors face sharply inward.

4. Covalent Bonding: Electroweak Sharing

In a covalent bond, two electrons share parity checks. Because electrons lack colour charge, the bond is mediated purely through the Electroweak sector ($I_3, \chi, W$). To bond, the two rings must orient face-to-face. Pauli exclusion dictates they must carry opposite embedding orientations (Spins ↑↓).

5. Orbitals & Entanglement Saturation

Atomic orbitals are not fundamental regions of space; they are probability clouds of syndrome updates mapping the electron's motion over the lattice.

6. Macroscopic Chemistry: The Water Molecule

The 104.5° bond angle of water is not an energetic compromise in continuous 3D space. It is the strict geometric consequence of aligning Oxygen's complex 24-quark nucleus onto the 2D cell complex to share parity checks with two inbound Hydrogen electron clouds.
