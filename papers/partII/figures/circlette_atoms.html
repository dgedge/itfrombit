Circlette Patterns: From Atoms to Molecules

Visual representations of how circlette codewords arrange on the lattice to form atoms and molecules. Each oriented ring represents a fundamental fermion; colours encode quantum properties; connections represent shared parity checks.

1. The Circlette Alphabet

The circlette alphabet. Ring colour = colour charge. Ring direction = matter (clockwise). Spin arrow = embedding orientation. Dashed = neutrino (zero charge, ghostly presence).

2. The Proton: A Colour-Neutral Triangle

The proton as a triangular cluster of three circlettes. Purple connections = shared colour parity checks (strong force). The composite XOR pattern 00100100 is one bit-flip from the electron.

3. Hydrogen Atom

Hydrogen atom. The proton's RGB triangle sits at the centre. The electron circlette occupies a diffuse cloud (the 1s orbital). Orange radial lines suggest the syndrome field (Coulomb interaction) binding them. Dashed ghost electrons show the probability distribution.

4. Helium Atom

Helium atom. Dense nuclear cluster of 12 quark circlettes (solid = proton quarks, dashed = neutron quarks). Two electrons in the 1s cloud with opposite embedding orientations (spin ↑↓), necessarily entangled in a singlet. The "FULL" label marks the exhausted entanglement capacity — hence chemical inertness.

5. Water Molecule (H₂O)

Water molecule. Each atom is a nuclear cluster (RGB triangles) surrounded by electron circlette clouds. Covalent bonds (gold dashed ellipses) are regions where electron codewords from different atoms share parity checks — entanglement across the bond. Lone pairs (top) are filled ↑↓ pairs with no remaining bonding capacity. The 104.5° bond angle emerges from the geometry of how parity checks distribute on the lattice.
